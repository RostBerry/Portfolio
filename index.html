Rostislavs has a long story with chess and chess programming in particular. It all began when he wanted to embrace the might of Python and test his skills by writing a chess bot that could conquer the lands of the Stockfish - the best chess bot on the planet at the moment. It took him 2 months to write just a game without the bot and to realize that his code was inefficient and contained lots of unfixable bugs, so he decided to rewrite it from scratch using a more standardized approach and more optimized techniques. He spent another couple of months and was very happy because of the drastic optimizations he did on the way. Eventually, he completed the game and even ran the first version of the bot, and it was so slow that scanning 2 moves deep into the position would take half a minute, and finally realized that Python is just a piece of garbage so he decided to rewrite it once again, using C#. From that point, Rostislavs accumulated all of the chess programming science with its most advanced techniques into his project and spent another 4 months making a blank engine without a bot, so scanning 2 moves deep into position would take the bot 1800 ns instead of 30 seconds. At first, it looked like a complete success, but if you remember, Rostislavs wanted to pair this bot with Stockfish in a death fight, and Stockfish could scan 2 moves deep into the position in 110 ns, it looked more like a RAM latency. It means that Rostislavs' bot was ~18x slower than Stockfish so he decided to rewrite it once again, into the final revision of the bot. You can tell he is desperate because now he has chosen to use Rust, which means a lot.
